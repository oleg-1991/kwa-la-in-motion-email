LA In Motion

 
 

Looking Back

LA doesn’t slow down, and this year proved it in a big way. Looking back at 2025 — and ahead to what’s next — I’m struck by the sheer scale of what Los Angeles is taking on.

In fire-torn neighborhoods, entire communities are undertaking the enormous effort of rebuilding — one permit, one home, one street at a time.

At the same time, the city is advancing transformative projects in preparation for the 2028 Olympic and Paralympic Games, investing billions in airports, convention centers, transportation networks, and cultural destinations.

While both efforts are immense, one is personal and local; the other sweeping and international. And in both arenas, KW&A has been working behind the scenes, clearing hurdles, guiding projects to the finish line.

From wildfire recovery to the global spotlight, we’re helping Los Angeles rise to the challenge, shaping not only the skyline but also the way the city moves, lives, and recovers.

 
 
 

Looking Forward

Rebuilding

The shock and devastation of the Palisades and Eaton wildfires left our city reeling. But it wasn’t long before I started receiving calls from friends and clients asking how quickly they could rebuild and how we could help.

That determination was incredibly energizing.

We mobilized immediately, and from that moment on, our focus has been on putting KW&A’s relationships to work — leveraging partnerships with government agencies, city departments, and elected officials to accelerate recovery. Together, we drove efforts to streamline approvals, remove grading bond requirements, and extend Executive Order 8 relief to municipal and commercial projects.

In some cases, thanks to heavy upfront due diligence, we helped secure permits in as little as 30 days while advocating for property owners across single-family, commercial, institutional, and municipal projects. We remain committed to supporting property owners rebuild with confidence.

Redeveloping

Further inland, we were part of an amazing team redeveloping Echelon Television Center, once home to Technicolor. Spanning two city blocks, the 620,000-square-foot campus includes soundstages, creative workspaces, bungalows, a basecamp, and terraces with unobstructed views of the Hollywood Hills. Designed to support the full spectrum of content creation, this spectacular project reinforces Hollywood as the beating heart of the world’s entertainment capital.

 
 
 
 

Readying

We’re helping LA get game-ready for the 2028 Olympic and Paralympic Games, advancing nearly $25 billion in projects.

 
 
 
 
 

From major upgrades at LAX, including the Airfield & Terminal Modernization Project, Automated People Mover, Consolidated Rent-A-Car Facility, Delta’s Terminals 2 and 3 renovations, tenant improvements, and the cool and funky LAX Sign & Gateway Pylons project, to the transformation of the La Brea Tar Pits and the Convention Center, these projects are designed to modernize infrastructure, enhance the visitor experience, and showcase Los Angeles on the world stage. We’re proud to play a role in this historic transformation.

 
 
 
 
 
 

Revamping

In other sports news, we were thrilled to support the Dodgers in delivering a generational upgrade to their clubhouse. Fresh off another World Series win, it was an honor to help create a space worthy of champions. Go Dodgers!

 
 
 

Reimagining

Another project close to our hearts is William Mead Homes — one of LA’s oldest public housing communities and now one of its most exciting opportunities.

Built in the 1940s, the site has provided affordable housing for generations of Angelenos. But at 80 years old, it is long overdue for renewal.

We’re honored to help deliver a plan that expands and modernizes affordable housing, invests in neighborhood amenities, and delivers programs supporting residents’ health, employment, and education. It’s projects like these that fuel our purpose.

Giving Back

Beyond our work helping shape the built environment, I care deeply about the work that builds connection and community. I’m grateful to support organizations doing truly meaningful work and proud to serve on their boards.

La Charla is an extraordinary program empowering teens in rural Nicaragua through peer-led health education and university scholarships. Before it began, not a single student from the village had gone to university. Today, more than 75 students have earned scholarships, and the program is now part of the core curriculum for youth ages 12–18.

Kindness Adventures offers a unique kind of journey — a women’s travel collective that blends adventure with hands-on community service. It’s about connection, impact, and leading with compassion, proving that kindness can be a form of action.

And closer to home, the Burbank YMCA continues to strengthen youth, families, and community wellness through programs that support body, mind, and spirit.

If you’d like to support any of these efforts, or jump on a service adventure, please reach out to me. Let’s do some more great things together!
